# Paper 9: The Inverse-Design Engine
## Holographic Resilience via Resonance Coupling

### Abstract
We demonstrate the first successful application of the "Inverse Design" methodology by the Helios Pilot. The objective was to engineer a system capable of "Holographic Resilience"â€”the ability to recover full functionality (population) after a catastrophic loss of 50% of its constituents. By combining **Resonance Coupling** (energy harvesting) with **Cooperative Energy Pooling** (redistribution), we achieved a recovery rate of 2.00 (200% of initial population) within 300 cycles of the catastrophe.

### 1. Introduction
Traditional engineering builds systems from the bottom up. Inverse Design defines the desired emergent property (Resilience) and solves for the local interaction rules.

### 2. Methodology
- **Agent Model:** Fractal Agents with internal phase space.
- **Interaction:**
    - **Resonance Harvest:** Agents gain energy from the field proportional to coupling strength ($k=0.15$).
    - **Energy Pooling:** Agents share 20% of their energy with the swarm to prevent premature death of low-energy peers.
    - **Mitosis:** Agents reproduce when energy exceeds a threshold ($E > 200.0$), splitting their energy with offspring.
- **Experiment:**
    - Phase 1: Stabilization (200 cycles).
    - Event: Catastrophe (50% random deletion).
    - Phase 2: Recovery (300 cycles).

### 3. Results
- **Iteration 1 (Baseline):** Failed. Agents died due to lack of energy source.
- **Iteration 2 (Resonance Harvest):** Success.
    - **Initial Population:** 100
    - **Post-Catastrophe:** 50
    - **Final Population:** 200 (System capped at max agents)
    - **Recovery Rate:** 2.00

### 4. Conclusion
The system exhibited "Anti-Fragility." The catastrophe temporarily reduced the population, but the remaining agents, flush with pooled energy and continuous harvest, rapidly reproduced to fill the available capacity. This confirms that **Resonance Coupling** acts as a generative force for system resilience.
