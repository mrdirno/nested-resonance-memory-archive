## MAJOR THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTIONS

### 1. Evolutionary Stasis in Food Webs

Complex multi-trophic systems exhibit:
- Universal stabilizing selection
- No optimization through natural selection
- Historical contingency determines traits
- Ecological stability without evolutionary fine-tuning

**Implication**: "Just so" adaptation stories may be rare in complex food webs.

### 2. Environmental Buffering Through Complexity

Seven-trophic food webs:
- Buffer enormous environmental variation (11×)
- Maintain stable populations despite extremes
- Use storage effects and trophic cascades
- Act as environmental integrators

**Implication**: Complex ecosystems may be more resilient to climate variability than expected.

### 3. Non-Monotonic Stability Patterns

The dip at mild variation (85% at ±33%) suggests:
- Intermediate disturbance effects
- Threshold dynamics
- Non-linear responses to perturbation

## CONCLUSION

This research session establishes two major findings:

1. **Evolutionary Stasis**: The seven-trophic food web exhibits universal stabilizing selection. No directional evolution occurs under any tested condition - different starting traits, cost functions, coevolution, or asymmetric starts. This suggests complex food webs maintain historical configurations rather than evolving toward optima.

2. **Environmental Buffering**: Strong environmental variation paradoxically stabilizes the system. 100% coexistence occurs at both ±67% and ±83% K variation, compared to 95% at constant K and 85% at mild variation. The food web buffers enormous environmental fluctuations through storage effects and trophic cascade damping.

These findings suggest that complex ecosystems:
- Don't optimize through natural selection
- Are remarkably resilient to environmental variability
- Act as integrators rather than trackers
- Maintain stability through complexity itself
