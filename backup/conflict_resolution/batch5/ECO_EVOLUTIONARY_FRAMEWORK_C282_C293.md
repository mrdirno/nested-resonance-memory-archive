## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Density-Dependent Equilibrium

**Formula:** N* = K × (f_intra / df)

Validated across:
- Scales (N=2 to N=20)
- Fluctuations (50% K variation)
- Catastrophes (90% population loss)

### 2. Metapopulation Effects

**Migration:**
- Synchronizes populations (80% variance reduction)
- Rescues inferior competitors (binary transition)
- Equalizes fitness advantages

**Topology:**
- Distributed > centralized
- Ring sufficient, star creates bottleneck

**Direction:**
- Population ratio tracks migration ratio
- Asymmetry reduces total

### 3. Environmental Robustness

**Fluctuation:** Mean robust, variance increases
**Catastrophe:** Complete resilience (100% survival at 90% loss)
**Allee:** Critical threshold exists but stochastic

### 4. Evolutionary Dynamics

**Single population:**
- Selection produces massive fitness increase (20×)
- Neutral drift shows positive bias

**Two populations:**
- Evolver defeats non-evolver (100%)
- Both evolve → arms race → exclusion

## EMERGENT PHENOMENA

### 1. Scale Invariance

Local dynamics determine local equilibrium.
Total = N × local equilibrium.

### 2. Environmental Buffering

Density dependence acts as negative feedback.
Mean robust, variance controllable.

### 3. Catastrophic Resilience

No history dependence in equilibrium.
Complete recovery from any non-zero population.

### 4. Evolutionary Runaway

Higher fitness → more offspring → faster evolution.
Positive feedback leads to exclusion.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### For Stable Ecology
- Density dependence (df > 0)
- Distributed topology (no hubs)
- Bidirectional migration
- Avoid strong Allee effects

### For Diversity
- Local K (resource partitioning)
- Any migration (rescue effect)
- Niche differentiation (prevent exclusion)

### For Evolution
- Selection > 0
- Heritable variation
- Sufficient population size
