## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Shared predation equalizes prey populations regardless of carrying capacity.**

- C311: Identical prey (K=300 each) → 150:150:30
- C312: Differentiated prey (K=200 vs K=400) → 150:150:30

Predator creates neutral apparent competition.

---

## RESEARCH QUESTION

Does a shared predator cause competitive exclusion between prey species?

---

## RESULTS

| Cycle | Prey A K | Prey B K | Prey A Final | Prey B Final | Outcome |
|-------|----------|----------|--------------|--------------|---------|
| C311 | 300 | 300 | 150 | 150 | Both coexist |
| C312 | 200 | 400 | 150 | 150 | Both coexist |

100% coexistence in both cases. No exclusion.

---

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. No Competitive Exclusion

Despite different carrying capacities:
- Prey A (K=200): Expected equilibrium ~100
- Prey B (K=400): Expected equilibrium ~200

Both ended at 150 - predator equalizes.

### 2. Density-Dependent Equalization

Predator takes from larger population more:
- If Prey B grows larger (higher K), it gets more predation
- Negative feedback equalizes populations
- Neither excluded

### 3. Predator Population Constant

Same predator population (30) across conditions:
- Total prey biomass constant
- System self-regulates

---

## MECHANISM

### Apparent Competition → Neutral Coexistence

```
Without differentiation:
  Both prey at K/2 = 150

With differentiation:
  Expected: A at 100, B at 200
  Actual: Both at 150

Why? Predator predation is density-dependent:
  - Larger population = higher encounter rate
  - Higher predation = reduced population
  - Feedback equalizes
```

### Type II Equalization

Type II functional response creates proportional predation:
- Total prey pool → total predation rate
- Each species gets predation ∝ abundance
- Equilibrium: both at same population

## THEORETICAL SIGNIFICANCE

### 1. Apparent Competition is Neutral Here

Classic apparent competition theory:
- Two prey competing through shared predator
- Expected: one excludes the other

NRM result:
- Both coexist
- Density-dependent predation equalizes

### 2. Carrying Capacity Masked

Prey K differences masked by predation:
- K=200 vs K=400 → both at 150
- Predator creates "ceiling" below both K values
- Individual K irrelevant at equilibrium

### 3. Predator-Mediated Coexistence

Predator enables coexistence:
- Without predator: species at different levels
- With predator: both equalized
- Predator is "equalizer"
