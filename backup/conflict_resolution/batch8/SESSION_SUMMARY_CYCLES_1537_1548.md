## MAJOR DISCOVERIES

### 1. Food Chain Length Extends to Five Levels

Linear food chains stable through five levels:
- L0: 300 (Prey)
- L1: 30 (Mesopredator)
- L2: 10 (Top predator)
- L3: 5 (Apex)
- L4: 3 (Hyper)

No inherent limit found; top consumer at demographic threshold.

### 2. Omnivory Creates Bifurcation Instability

Same parameters gave opposite results with different seeds:
- Linear chain: 100% stable
- Food web: 0-100% (stochastic)

Web structure destabilizes compared to linear chains.

### 3. Predator Preference Has Optimal Zone

Preference threshold between 1.5× and 2×:
- 1.5× (0.0045): Sustainable
- 2.0× (0.006): Predator extinction

Strong preference is self-destructive.

### 4. Predators Equalize Prey Populations

Shared predation equalizes prey regardless of K:
- K=200 vs K=400 → both at 150
- Density-dependent predation creates equilibrium
- No competitive exclusion

### 5. Cascades Require Narrow Parameter Window

At optimal parameters, no cascade occurs:
- System is bottom-up regulated
- Top predator removal neutral
- Cascades need specific conditions

## THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

### Food Chain Structure

**Linear chains optimal for stability:**
- No cross-level feeding
- Each predator specializes
- Type II at all levels
- Parameters in viable zone

**Food webs reduce stability:**
- Omnivory creates competition
- Multiple links enable overexploitation
- Bifurcation near boundaries

### Goldilocks Principle Applies Throughout

**Attack rate:**
- 0.002-0.003: Optimal
- >0.006: Overexploitation

**Preference ratio:**
- 1.0-1.5×: Stable
- >2×: Self-destructive

**Chain length:**
- 3-5 levels: Achievable
- >5: Top consumer at threshold

### Energy Flow and Efficiency

Trophic efficiency decreases with level:
- L0→L1: ~10%
- L1→L2: ~33%
- L2→L3: ~50%
- L3→L4: ~60%

Each level supports ~1/10 to 1/3 of the level below.

## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. Simplicity Promotes Stability

Linear chains stable through five levels. Adding complexity (omnivory, webs) reduces stability. Simple hierarchical structure is optimal.

### 2. Goldilocks Applies Universally

Multiple parameters have optimal zones:
- Attack rate
- Preference ratio
- Chain length

Too much or too little causes problems.

### 3. Predators Are Equalizers

Shared predation creates negative feedback that:
- Equalizes prey populations
- Maintains diversity
- Prevents competitive exclusion

### 4. Top Consumers at Threshold

Fifth trophic level exists but:
- Only 3 individuals
- At demographic stochasticity risk
- May not persist long-term

### 5. Cascades Are Rare

At optimal parameters:
- Bottom-up regulation dominates
- Top predator removal is neutral
- Cascades need specific conditions
