### 7. Non-Monotonic Response Patterns and Intermediate Optima

**[26] Connell, J. H. (1978)**
*Diversity in tropical rain forests and coral reefs*
Science, 199(4335), 1302-1310
DOI: 10.1126/science.199.4335.1302
**Context:** Intermediate Disturbance Hypothesis - species diversity maximized at intermediate disturbance frequencies, creating U-shaped or hump-shaped response curves. Classic example of non-monotonic ecological patterns where intermediate values produce optimal outcomes. Conceptual parallel to observed non-monotonic spawn success across timescales (100% → 88% → 23%), where intermediate timescale (1000 cycles) shows near-maximum performance.
**Usage:** Section 4.X.1 (non-monotonic pattern interpretation), Section 1.4 (hypothesis motivation)

**[27] Tilman, D. (1982)**
*Resource Competition and Community Structure*
Monographs in Population Biology, 17. Princeton University Press, Princeton, NJ
ISBN: 978-0-691-08302-6
**Context:** Resource depletion and recovery dynamics in ecological communities. Describes how resource availability fluctuates through depletion-recovery cycles mediated by population density and consumption rates. Provides ecological parallel to population-mediated energy recovery mechanism where large populations distribute resource consumption load, allowing individual-level recovery.
**Usage:** Section 4.X.2 (energy recovery mechanism), Section 4.X.3 (distributed load balancing)

**[28] Charnov, E. L. (1976)**
*Optimal foraging, the marginal value theorem*
Theoretical Population Biology, 9(2), 129-136
DOI: 10.1016/0040-5809(76)90040-X
**Context:** Marginal value theorem describing optimal resource patch exploitation. Predicts organisms should leave resource patches when intake rate drops below environmental average. Relevant to understanding spawn selection dynamics where depleted agents are effectively "abandoned" in favor of non-depleted individuals, analogous to patch-switching in optimal foraging.
**Usage:** Section 4.X.2 (spawn selection as resource patch sampling), Section 4.X.5 (Self-Giving Systems connection - emergent optimization)
