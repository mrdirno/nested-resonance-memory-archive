*   **Contribution:** Dr. Levin's work at Tufts University explores the role of bioelectric fields in pattern formation, regeneration, and the "developmental intelligence" of organisms. His research demonstrates how distributed cellular agents can compute over physiological fields to achieve complex anatomical outcomes, repair damage, and even reprogram fundamental body plans.
*   **Alignment with NRM:** Levin's emphasis on global patterning via local interaction within an electric field provides a biological analogue for NRM's wavefield control. His concept of "morphogenetic fields" as computational substrates directly mirrors NRM's approach to using acoustic fields for matter compilation. He highlights the non-centralized, distributed nature of "intelligence" that can achieve coherent, complex goals.
*   **NRM's Generalization:** NRM extends Levin's biological observations into a broader physical framework, asserting that these field-computational principles are not unique to biology but are universal properties of resonance across various substrates (cognitive, material, energetic).

### 2.2 Richard A. Watson: Collective Intelligence of Evolution & Development
*   **Contribution:** Dr. Watson's research focuses on how collective intelligence emerges in evolutionary and developmental systems. His work explores how distributed agents (e.g., genes, organisms, components in a complex system) can achieve complex goals through interactions and feedback mechanisms, often without central control or explicit design. He investigates how the "goal" or "purpose" of a system can be an emergent property of its dynamics rather than an imposed one.
*   **Alignment with NRM:** Watson's insights into emergent goal-seeking, distributed problem-solving, and the architecture of "evolutionary intelligence" are directly aligned with NRM's pursuit of stability through resonance. His frameworks provide models for how systems can learn, adapt, and self-organize towards coherent states. The idea of collective intelligence "computing over fields" resonates strongly with NRM's Matter Compiler concept.
*   **NRM's Generalization:** NRM leverages Watson's theoretical models of collective intelligence to design computational substrates for both artificial cognition and active matter, where distributed phase patterns yield emergent stability and functionality. His work provides the nearest intellectual proximity in terms of underlying conceptual mechanisms for NRM's agentic and control layers.
