## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. Not All Papers Have Integration Opportunities

**Observation:**
- Paper 2 (energy dynamics): Natural integration point
- Paper 3 (temporal stewardship): No connection (orthogonal topic)

**Implication:**
- Don't force cross-references where themes don't align
- Quality over quantity (1 meaningful reference > 5 superficial)
- Expect ~40-60% of papers to have natural integration points

### 2. "Future Directions" is Optimal Integration Location

**Advantages:**
- Forward-looking context (naturally references other work)
- Minimal disruption to main narrative
- Reader expectation for cross-references
- Brief additions appropriate (1-2 sentences)

**Contrast with Results/Discussion:**
- Higher disruption
- Requires rewriting sections
- Risk of confusing main narrative
- Not worth cost unless strong thematic overlap

### 3. Cross-Reference Strengthens Both Papers

**For Paper 2:**
- Future work now points to existing work addressing it
- Demonstrates research program continuity
- Positions within broader framework
