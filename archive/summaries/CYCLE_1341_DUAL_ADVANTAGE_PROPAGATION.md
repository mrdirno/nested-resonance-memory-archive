## Lessons Learned

### Systematic Integration Protocol

**Successful approach:**
1. Grep for all mentions of key finding (α = 607)
2. Categorize by section (theory, applications, summary)
3. Update applications first (concrete examples)
4. Update summary last (synthesize changes)
5. Verify consistency (all mentions updated)

**Generalization:** When integrating new findings, work bottom-up (concrete → abstract) for clearer impact visualization.

### Multi-Dimensional Validation Strengthening

**Insight:** Adding stability dimension to efficiency finding:
- Doubles number of testable predictions per domain
- Increases falsifiability (two ways to fail instead of one)
- Strengthens application arguments (two independent benefits)
- Differentiates from classical theories (efficiency alone insufficient)

**Pattern:** Look for **orthogonal dimensions** of system behavior that can strengthen findings beyond single-metric improvements.

### Qualitative vs. Quantitative Framing

**Key distinction established:**
- Quantitative: Same kind of system, different magnitude (2× better, 607× better)
- Qualitative: Different category of system (efficient + stable vs. inefficient + unstable)

**Impact:** Qualitative framing positions hierarchical NRM as **novel category**, not incremental improvement.

**Lesson:** Frame breakthrough findings as **category changes**, not just **performance improvements**.
