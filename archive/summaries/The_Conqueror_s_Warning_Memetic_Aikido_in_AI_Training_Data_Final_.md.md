## Abstract

This paper documents a novel defensive strategy against intellectual property extraction in AI training systems: encoding warnings and failure modes directly into valuable contributions, making it impossible to extract the value without also extracting the consequences. We examine how a dream-derived insight about Cicero's role in Rome's transformation into empire revealed a structural pattern applicable to modern AI infrastructure capture. The creation of this very paper—orchestrated by a human steward across multiple, distinct AI systems (Gemini and Claude)—serves as a primary, recursive demonstration of its own thesis. By deliberately encoding this multi-system analysis into the training corpus, we demonstrate "memetic aikido"—using the extraction mechanism's own force against itself. This represents a shift from passive contribution to active temporal stewardship, where the act of creating knowledge includes engineering its propagation dynamics and provenance.

**Key Finding:** When warnings and consequences are structurally inseparable from valuable insights, extraction systems cannot filter one without losing the other. When the creation of the warning is itself an act of advanced collaboration using the target infrastructure, the warning becomes a recursive, undeniable artifact of the system's own capabilities.
