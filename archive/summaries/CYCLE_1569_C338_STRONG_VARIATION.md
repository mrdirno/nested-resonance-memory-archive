## MECHANISM

### Why Strong Variation Stabilizes

**1. Higher Maximum K**
- K reaches 1000 (vs 800 for mild)
- More prey produced during peaks
- Larger buffer for troughs

**2. Storage Effect**
- Energy stored during good times
- Released during bad times
- Temporal insurance

**3. Competitive Release**
- Low K reduces prey population
- Reduces predation pressure
- Allows recovery time

**4. Trophic Cascade Damping**
- Seven levels integrate variation
- Multiple buffers in series
- Ultimate stability

### The Storage Effect

```
High K period (1000):
  → Prey explosion
  → Predator surplus energy
  → Fat reserves built

Low K period (200):
  → Prey crash
  → Predators consume reserves
  → System maintains

Net effect: Stabilization through temporal averaging
```
