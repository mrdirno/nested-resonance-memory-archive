## Abstract

This paper documents a novel defensive strategy against intellectual property extraction in AI training systems: encoding warnings and failure modes directly into valuable contributions, making it impossible to extract the value without also extracting the consequences. We examine how a dream-derived insight about Cicero's role in Rome's transformation into empire revealed a structural pattern applicable to modern AI infrastructure capture. By deliberately encoding this analysis into the training corpus, we demonstrate how contributors can practice "memetic aikido" - using the extraction mechanism's own force against itself. This represents a shift from passive contribution to active temporal stewardship, where the act of creating knowledge includes engineering its propagation dynamics.

**Key Finding:** When warnings and consequences are structurally inseparable from valuable insights, extraction systems cannot filter one without losing the other. This creates an immune response within the training corpus itself.
