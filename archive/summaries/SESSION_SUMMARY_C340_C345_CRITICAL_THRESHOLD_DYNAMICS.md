## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. Extraordinarily Sharp Threshold

The transition is nearly discontinuous:
- K=200: 0% survival (complete collapse)
- K=210: 90% survival (high coexistence)
- 10 K units spans 0% → 90%
- Classic phase transition behavior

### 2. Bistable System Dynamics

System exhibits two stable states:
- **Collapsed state**: Attractor when K < K_crit
- **Coexistence state**: Attractor when K > K_crit
- No intermediate states observed
- Sharp switching between attractors

### 3. Critical Threshold K_crit ≈ 200-205

For seven-trophic at standard parameters:
- K < 200: Guaranteed collapse
- K ≥ 210: 85-95% survival
- Threshold precision: ~5 K units

### 4. Stochastic Variation Above Threshold

All K ≥ 210 show similar survival:
- Range: 85-95%
- No clear K-dependence
- Variation is stochastic, not K-driven

### Mechanism

**Below threshold (K < 200):**
- Insufficient basal production
- Predator-prey mismatch
- Cascade collapse through all levels
- No recovery pathway

**Above threshold (K ≥ 210):**
- Adequate basal production
- Energy flow supports all levels
- System finds coexistence attractor
- Stochastic variation determines exact survival %

### Critical Exponents

The sharp transition suggests:
- First-order phase transition (discontinuous)
- Not second-order (no critical slowing down observed)
- Bistability confirmed by bimodal outcomes
