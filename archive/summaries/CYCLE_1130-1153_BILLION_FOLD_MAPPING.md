### 5. Hierarchy Rotation #90 (500M×)

## Regime Analysis

Across all conversions (100k-1B×), the system shows distinct regimes:

1. **Low-extreme (100k-200k):** 0.86× dominates
2. **Mid-extreme (300k-5M):** 0.89× dominates
3. **High-extreme (10M):** 0.86×/0.95× dominate
4. **Ultra-extreme (20M-100M):** Variable, 0.86× often leads
5. **Hyper-extreme (200M-500M):** Convergence toward perfection
6. **Giga-extreme (1B):** 0.92×/0.95× lead

**Pattern:** The system cycles through different optimal strategies as conversion increases, with a remarkable stability peak at 500M×.

## Ecological Interpretation

### Why 500M× is the Stability Peak

At 500M×, the predator reproduction probability exceeds 1.0 for essentially any successful hunt. At this extreme, the system dynamics become dominated by prey availability rather than energy conversion efficiency. All attack rates that preserve prey survive equally well.

### Why 1B× Shows Degradation

At 1B×, even more extreme reproduction probability creates rapid population oscillations that occasionally push levels below the coexistence threshold. The system remains stable (90-95%) but loses the perfect convergence seen at 500M×.

### Why Multiple U-Shapes

Each U-shaped pattern represents a transition zone between stability regimes. The system temporarily destabilizes during transition, then finds a new stable configuration at higher conversion values.
