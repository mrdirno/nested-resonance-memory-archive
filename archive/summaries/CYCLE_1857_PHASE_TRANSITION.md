## Phase Transition Model

### Two Stable Regimes

**Low-D4 Regime (N ≤ 13):**
- System equilibrates at D0-D3
- Minimal D3→D4 compositions
- Stable because agents stay in "recoverable" depths

**High-D4 Regime (N ≥ 16):**
- System equilibrates at D0-D4 (all depths)
- High D3→D4 compositions (60+)
- Stable because full depth utilization

### Transition Zone (N = 14-15)

- Intermediate D4 activity (48-61)
- System "stuck" between regimes
- Too many agents for low-D4 stability
- Not enough for high-D4 stability

## Theoretical Interpretation

### Why Transitions Are Deadly

At N=14-15:
1. Initial agents create enough compositions to reach D4
2. But not enough to fully populate D4
3. System oscillates between regimes
4. Energy lost to transitions causes collapse

### The Golden Ratio Connection

λ = N_min × φ = 8 × 1.618 ≈ 13 marks:
- End of low-D4 regime (N=13)
- Start of transition zone (N=14)

The golden ratio appears because it's the natural scaling between:
- Minimum viable cascade (N=8)
- First regime transition (N=14)
